Hands-On 5 · Week 3 · Module M8 — Research with NotebookLM

Research Knowledge Base

Week 3 M8 · Research with NotebookLM Solo RAG
Done when
You have a NotebookLM knowledge base that answers questions from your own sources with citations — plus a briefing (and/or an Audio Overview). Real retrieval, no code, no API key.
What you build

A research knowledge base in Google NotebookLM: add real sources (docs, PDFs, web pages, videos), then ask questions that come back with a citation next to every claim. Because NotebookLM retrieves the relevant passage and generates an answer grounded in it, this is genuine RAG — the honest step up from HO4's in-context grounding (pasting text into a prompt), not retrieval. You then generate a study guide / FAQ / briefing and, optionally, an Audio Overview (a two-host podcast of your sources). Subscription-first — free with a Google account, with your Claude.ai plan as the planning/polishing partner.

Pick a problem statement

Each sample is a real, worked build — the source list, the cited Q&A and a briefing all filled in. Open one live, or bring your own research question (get it approved first).

Use your Claude.ai Pro plan wisely
Subscription-first — no API key
  • NotebookLM is free and separate, so most of this hands-on won't touch your Claude limit at all.
  • Use the example prompts and questions in each sample's README — they're already written and tested.
  • One clear message beats lots of vague back-and-forth.
  • Start a new chat when you switch tasks — long chats re-read every earlier message and use up your limit faster.
  • Don't paste big files over and over. Paste once, then refer back to it.
