# Gap-Analysis Brief — TaskFlow vs. Plannr, GridWork, Kanbanly

## Bottom line
All three rivals have a **clear, citable weakness TaskFlow can attack**: Plannr is slow at scale, GridWork is hard for non-technical users, and Kanbanly is boxed in by API and offline limits. None of these are marketing spin — each comes from third-party reviews or the competitor's own docs.

| **GridWork** | "A spreadsheet your team will love" | Confuses non-technical teammates; automations fail silently | `[S5]` vs `[S6][S7]` |

## Pricing angle
For a small (3-person) team, Plannr is cheapest but caps projects at 3 `[S4]`; GridWork forces a 5-seat minimum `[S8]`; Kanbanly's free tier stops at 2 boards `[S12]`. **Opportunity:** a no-seat-minimum, unlimited-projects entry plan undercuts all three.

## Recommended positioning
1. **Speed at scale** — "Fast even on 1,000-task boards." (beats Plannr `[S3]`)
2. **Zero learning curve** — "Usable day one, no spreadsheet skills." (beats GridWork `[S7]`)
3. **No integration ceilings** — "Unlimited API calls on every paid plan." (beats Kanbanly `[S11]`)
