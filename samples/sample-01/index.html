Competitor Intelligence Knowledge Base

Week 3 M9 · Research & RAG Solo Copy-and-use template
Problem statement
As a marketer you can't keep up with what competitors are shipping. Build a NotebookLM knowledge base over their sites, launch posts, reviews and docs, then ask “what are their three biggest weaknesses?” and get answers that cite the exact source. Success: a notebook + a cited gap-analysis brief + an Audio Overview.
Copy-and-use template

This folder comes with a worked example already filled in, plus clearly-marked << … >> blanks for your own material. Clone it, swap in your real sources, and you have your knowledge base. For the fully-worked reference, switch to the main branch.

What you build
A research knowledge base in Google NotebookLM. You add your competitors' real sources — marketing pages, launch blog posts, G2/Reddit reviews, help docs, pricing pages. NotebookLM reads only those sources and answers with a citation next to every claim, so you can click through and check it came from a real place. That's the honest difference from a normal chatbot: this is retrieval + generation (RAG) over your material, not the model guessing from memory. Worked reference: a fictional PM-software company, TaskFlow, sizing up three rivals — swap in your own real competitors.
What ships in this folder
sources.md

The exact list of sources that went into the notebook.

 qa.md

Real questions with the cited answers NotebookLM gave.

 briefing.md

A one-page briefing / study guide you could hand over.
Build it in NotebookLM
No API key, no code — NotebookLM is free with a Google account.

Go to notebooklm.google.com, sign in, and click Create new notebook.

Click Add source — paste a website URL, upload a PDF, paste text, or add a YouTube link. Aim for 6–12 good sources across 2–3 competitors.

Ask a real question, e.g. “What are the three biggest weaknesses customers complain about for each competitor? Cite the source for each.”

Every claim has a numbered citation — click one to jump to the exact sentence. Save the good Q&A into qa.md.

In Studio, generate a Briefing doc and copy it into briefing.md; then Audio Overview → Generate for a two-host podcast of your sources.

Where Claude.ai fits
  • Ask Claude to help you choose which sources to add and write sharp questions to ask NotebookLM.
  • Paste NotebookLM's cited answers into Claude and ask it to tighten the gap-analysis brief for a specific audience (e.g. your sales team).
How this differs from HO4

HO4 was in-context grounding — you pasted text into a prompt and the model answered from that text. HO5 is real retrieval: NotebookLM stores many sources, finds the relevant passages for each question, and cites them. That retrieval step is what makes this genuine RAG.
