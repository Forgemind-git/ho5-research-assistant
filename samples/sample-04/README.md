# Sample 04 — Exam / Certification Study KB (copy-and-use template)

> A ready-to-adapt template for a NotebookLM knowledge base over your syllabus and dense readings — it generates a **study guide** and **FAQ** and answers revision questions with **cited** answers you can trust.

**This is a template** with a worked AWS-exam example plus `<< … >>` blanks for your own course.

## The problem statement

> *"You're revising for an exam or certification from a syllabus and dense readings. Build a NotebookLM knowledge base over those materials, generate a study guide and FAQ, and ask questions that return cited answers you can trust. Success: a notebook with a study guide + 5 cited Q&A + an Audio Overview, documented in your repo."*

## Use it with your Claude.ai subscription

No API key, no code.

1. Go to **[notebooklm.google.com](https://notebooklm.google.com)**, sign in, **Create new notebook**.
2. **Add source**: upload the **exam guide PDF**, the **syllabus**, and your key **readings/whitepapers** (PDF or URL). Add lecture notes as **pasted text** (see [`sources.md`](./sources.md)).
3. In **Studio**, click **Study guide** to generate one, and **FAQ** for a self-test sheet. Copy the study guide into [`briefing.md`](./briefing.md).
4. Ask the revision questions in [`qa.md`](./qa.md), e.g. *"When should I use X vs Y? Cite the reading."* Check the **citation**, then paste answers in.
5. Generate an **Audio Overview** to revise on the go; **Share** for a link.
6. (Optional) **Share** the notebook with your study group.

**No API key needed.** Genuine **RAG** — NotebookLM retrieves across your whole reading list and cites the source, so a revision answer is always checkable against the real material (unlike HO4's single pasted passage).

## The three template files
- **`sources.md`** — source-list template (exam guide + syllabus + readings; example rows + blanks).
- **`qa.md`** — five revision questions with one worked answer and blanks.
- **`briefing.md`** — a one-page **study guide** template plus FAQ pointers.

## Make it your own
- The **exam guide + 3–6 core readings** is a strong start. Split giant whitepapers into the chapters you need.
- Use the **Study guide** and **FAQ** buttons first — they turn dense readings into a revision plan in seconds.
- Ask "explain X like I'm new, then cite where to read more" to pair a plain explanation with a trustworthy source.
