Industry Reports Knowledge Base

Week 3 M9 · Research & RAG Solo Copy-and-use template
Problem statement
Your industry publishes reports faster than you can read them. Build a NotebookLM knowledge base over the latest reports in your field and ask “what changed this quarter and why does it matter for us?” with cited answers. Success: a notebook + a cited briefing you could send to your team + an Audio Overview.
Copy-and-use template

This folder comes with a worked example already filled in, plus clearly-marked << … >> blanks for your own material. Clone it, swap in your real sources, and you have your knowledge base. For the fully-worked reference, switch to the main branch.

What you build
A research knowledge base over the stack of quarterly reports you never have time to read. You load the latest industry reports, benchmark studies and trend decks, then ask the one question that matters — “what changed this quarter, and what should we do about it?” — and get an answer that cites the exact report and page. So the briefing you forward is built on sourced facts, not vibes. For this reference we're a digital-marketing team tracking martech and channel-performance reports, but the shape works for any field. Genuine RAG: “email open rates fell 4%” links straight to the report that says so.
What ships in this folder
sources.md

The exact list of sources that went into the notebook.

 qa.md

Real questions with the cited answers NotebookLM gave.

 briefing.md

A one-page briefing / study guide you could hand over.
Build it in NotebookLM
No API key, no code — NotebookLM is free with a Google account.

Go to notebooklm.google.com, sign in, Create new notebook.

Add source: upload each report PDF, or paste the report URL, or add a YouTube analyst briefing. Add 4–8 recent reports.

Ask: “What are the three biggest changes across these reports this quarter, and why does each matter for a mid-size marketing team? Cite each.”

Verify the citations point at the right report/page. Save into qa.md.

In Studio, generate a Briefing doc into briefing.md and sharpen it into a “for the team” note; generate an Audio Overview for a Monday-morning listen.

Where Claude.ai fits
  • Ask Claude to help you pick which reports are worth adding and frame the “so what for us” question for your specific team.
  • Paste NotebookLM's cited findings into Claude to draft the team email or Slack post — the citations keep it credible.
How this differs from HO4

HO4 answered from one pasted document. Here NotebookLM retrieves across a pile of long reports at once, finds what changed, and cites the report it came from — the retrieval + citation is what lets you forward the briefing with confidence.
