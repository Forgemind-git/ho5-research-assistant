### Q1. "What are the three biggest changes this quarter, and why does each matter for a mid-size marketing team?"

**Answer:**
1. **AI Overviews are cutting organic search CTR** — the search report shows top-position clicks down noticeably where AI Overviews appear **[S4]**. *Matters because* traffic you used to get for free is shrinking — lean harder on branded/bottom-funnel terms.
2. **Paid-social CPMs rose** quarter-on-quarter while CTR was flat **[S2]**. *Matters because* the same budget buys fewer impressions — creative efficiency now drives ROI more than spend.
3. **AI tooling adoption in martech jumped**, especially for content and analytics **[S3]**. *Matters because* competitors are shipping content faster — a tooling gap becomes an output gap.

---

### Q2. "Did email performance improve or decline this quarter?"

**Answer:** Mixed. **Open rates dipped slightly** (attributed partly to privacy/prefetch noise), but **click-to-open rates held or improved** for segmented campaigns **[S1]**. Deliverability tightened — the report flags stricter sender requirements **[S1]**. Takeaway: segmentation is protecting engagement even as raw opens get noisier **[S1]**.

---

### Q3. "Which channel got more expensive, and by how much?"

**Answer:** **Paid social** is the clear cost riser: CPMs increased quarter-on-quarter across the major platforms, with CTR roughly flat, so effective cost-per-click rose **[S2]**. The ad-platform briefing attributes part of it to election-period and holiday-run-up demand **[S6]**. Search CPCs were comparatively stable **[S4]**.

---

### Q4. "What does the data say about AI's impact on our work specifically?"

**Answer:** Two things. On **output**, martech AI adoption rose sharply, with content generation the top use case **[S3]**. On **traffic**, AI Overviews in search are **reducing organic CTR** for informational queries **[S4]**. Net for a marketing team: use AI to produce more, but expect informational SEO traffic to fall and rebalance toward branded and conversion content **[S3][S4]**.

---

### Q5. "Are conversion rates up or down, and where should we focus?"

**Answer:** CRO benchmarks were **broadly flat, with wide variance by industry** — e-commerce roughly steady, lead-gen slightly down **[S7]**. The report ties the best-performing sites to faster load times and clearer CTAs **[S7]**. Given flat conversion but rising acquisition costs `[S2]`, the highest-leverage focus this quarter is **conversion-rate optimisation**, not more top-of-funnel spend **[S7]**.
